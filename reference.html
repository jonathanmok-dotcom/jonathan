References

Every project should give credit to the sources that inspired and informed it. For this Brawl Stars fan site, I relied on a mix of official resources and community-driven platforms to ensure accuracy and depth. The official Supercell website provided the foundation, offering details about the game’s history, updates, and developer insights. The Brawl Stars Wiki was invaluable for learning about each brawler’s abilities, gadgets, star powers, and roles in different modes. YouTube gameplay videos helped me understand strategies, map control, and how professional players approach matches. The official Brawl Stars blog gave me access to patch notes and seasonal event information, which kept the content up to date.

By combining these sources, I was able to create a site that not only celebrates the fun of Brawl Stars but also educates new players about its mechanics. References are important because they show respect for the creators and communities that make the game thrive. They also allow readers to explore further if they want more detail. Each link provides a deeper look into the world of Brawl Stars, from official announcements to community strategies. Together, they form the backbone of this fan site and ensure that the information presented is reliable and engaging.

In addition to official sources, community contributions such as fan guides, Reddit discussions, and Discord groups have provided valuable insights. These platforms highlight strategies, showcase creative fan art, and share personal experiences that enrich the overall Brawl Stars culture. By acknowledging both official and community sources, this project reflects the collaborative spirit that makes Brawl Stars more than just a game — it’s a global community of players and creators.
